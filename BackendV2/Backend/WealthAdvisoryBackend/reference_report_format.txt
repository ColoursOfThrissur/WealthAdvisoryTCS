Market Commentary

Q4 2025 Market Review:
Global equity markets posted positive returns in the fourth quarter of 2025, with US large-cap stocks advancing 
modestly as investors balanced resilient corporate earnings against concerns about the Federal Reserve's path 
toward interest rate normalization. The S&P 500 gained +2.4% for the quarter, capping a strong full-year gain 
of +23.3%.

Technology and consumer discretionary sectors led equity performance during Q4, while utilities and real estate 
lagged as longer-duration assets faced headwinds from still-elevated interest rates. International developed 
markets (as measured by MSCI EAFE) gained +1.2% for the quarter, modestly underperforming US equities, partly 
due to dollar strength and slower economic momentum in Europe and Japan.

Portfolio Impact:
The portfolio's overweight position in domestic mid-cap equities (MDY +4.1%) and technology via the Invesco QQQ 
Trust (QQQ +5.1%) were the primary contributors to outperformance relative to the benchmark during the quarter. 
The fixed income sleeve was a modest drag — investment-grade bonds (AGG -0.4%) and international bonds (BNDX -0.6%) 
both posted small declines as yields drifted higher.

Outlook & Positioning:
Looking into 2026, we remain constructively positioned in US equities while being mindful of elevated valuations 
in large-cap growth. The Federal Reserve is expected to maintain a cautious approach to rate cuts, which we believe 
supports quality-oriented, shorter-duration fixed income and selective exposure to real assets. We will conduct a 
formal rebalance in March 2026 to bring the portfolio back in line with target weights.

We continue to recommend holding 5% in cash and short-term equivalents, which provides both a liquidity buffer and 
optionality to redeploy capital should volatility create more attractive entry points in either equities or fixed income.
